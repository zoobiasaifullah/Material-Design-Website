Philosophy

What is Material Design?

A comprehensive design system that synthesizes classic principles of good design with innovation and technology

palette

Material is the Metaphor

A visual language grounded in the principles of good design combined with innovation and technology. Inspired by the physical world and tactile reality, Material surfaces reimagine the mediums of paper and ink.

star

Bold, Graphic, Intentional

Print-based design elements—typography, grids, space, scale, color, and imagery—guide visual treatments. These elements create hierarchy, meaning, and focus that immerse users in the experience.

motion_photos_on

Motion Provides Meaning

Motion respects and reinforces the user as the prime mover. Primary user actions are inflection points that initiate motion, transforming the whole design with choreographed transitions.

Core Concepts

Design Principles

The foundational principles that guide every decision in Material Design

01

Create a Visual Language

We challenged ourselves to create a visual language for our users that synthesizes the classic principles of good design with the innovation and possibility of technology and science.

02

Develop a Unified System

Develop a single underlying system that allows for a unified experience across platforms and device sizes. Mobile precepts are fundamental, but touch, voice, mouse, and keyboard are all first-class input methods.

03

Adaptable Foundation

The Material Design system is designed to enable brand expression. It's built to be a living, evolving system that grows with our understanding of great design and technology.

04

Meaningful Motion

Motion provides meaning about the spatial relationships, functionality, and intention of the system. It focuses attention, maintains continuity, and provides subtle feedback.
